Sub Bass

20-60 Hz
The lowest frequencies we can hear and feel. Kick drum fundamental, bass guitar lowest notes, synth bass.
Too Much:
Muddy, boomy, loss of clarity
Too Little:
Thin, weak low end

Bass

60-250 Hz
Warmth and body of most instruments. Kick drum body, bass guitar main tone, low toms, male vocal chest.
Too Much:
Muddy, cloudy, indistinct
Too Little:
Thin, weak, lacking warmth

Low Mids

250-500 Hz
Fullness and body. Snare body, guitar body, vocal warmth.
Too Much:
Boxy, honky, telephone-like
Too Little:
Hollow, distant

Mids

500 Hz - 2 kHz
Presence and definition of most instruments. Vocal clarity, guitar presence, snare crack.
Too Much:
Harsh, nasal, fatiguing
Too Little:
Distant, recessed, lacks presence

Upper Mids

2-4 kHz
Attack, clarity, and intelligibility. Vocal consonants, cymbal definition, string attack.
Too Much:
Harsh, piercing, painful
Too Little:
Dull, muffled, lacks definition

Presence

4-6 kHz
Clarity and "forward" sound. Vocal intelligibility, cymbal shimmer.
Too Much:
Sibilant, harsh, ear fatigue
Too Little:
Dull, buried in the mix

Brilliance

6-20 kHz
Air, sparkle, and shimmer. Cymbals, acoustic guitar shimmer, vocal breathiness.
Too Much:
Brittle, harsh, sibilant
Too Little:
Dull, lifeless, flat
